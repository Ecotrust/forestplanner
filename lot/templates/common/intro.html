Get Started

Welcome

On the Forest Planner website, you will be guided through four major steps.  Completing these steps will allow you to do side-by-side comparisons of different management scenarios for your property:

  1. Step 1: Map your property boundary

  2. Step 2: Map the management units (forest stands) within your property

  3. Step 3: Describe the type of forest found in each stand

  4. Step 4: Create and visualize one or more property-level management scenarios


Step 1: Map your property boundary.

You will have two options to draw your property boundary:

  1. Option 1: Draw your property boundary by hand.

  2. Option 2: Upload a GIS shapefile with your property and forest stands.

    Some pre-formatting is required (.zip file, web mercator projection)

Step 2: Map the management units (forest stands) within your property

You will have two options to divide your property into forest stands:

  1. Option 1: Draw your stand boundaries by hand.

  2. Option 2: Upload GIS data

    (stands are automatically mapped from the shapefile you uploaded to define your property boundary).

Step 3: Describe the type of forest found in each stand

You will define each type of forest that occurs on your property and then apply them to each stand on your property.

  1. For each forest type you create, you will give a name, age, and a list of the tree species, diameter class, and number of trees per acre.
  2. All the forest types you have created will appear like a painter’s palette to the left of your stand map.  Click on one or more stands in the map, and then apply the appropriate forest type.

Step 4: Create and visualize one or more property-level management scenarios

Each scenario represents the combination of management prescriptions you choose to apply to each stand across your property. To create each scenario, you will:

  1. Have the option to visualize map layers that may affect your management choices, such as riparian buffers, steep slopes, etc.
  2. Create a list of the silvicultural prescriptions you would like to apply to your stands.
  3. Select each stand on your property and apply the prescription you want.
  4. Give your scenario a name, and description (optional).

Once you have completed these 4 steps, you will be able to see side-by-side graphs and maps comparing any management scenarios you created.

For example, you can see how many boardfeet would be harvested under each scenario, or how much carbon would be stored in your trees.  You can also compare estimates of some of the major costs and revenues in each scenario.

You can also see maps that will show you timber stocking, yields, carbons storage, etc. for a snapshot in time over the next 100 years.

If at any point you are not sure what to do, check in the information box to see advice and links to short videos showing how to use that page.
